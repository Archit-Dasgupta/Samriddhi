The Challenge

Rural women want to earn more but lack the tools and trust to start. Only 27% of Indians are financially literate (rural: ~23%, vs global 42%). Women in rural Maharashtra who wish to start or scale micro-businesses face low confidence, dependence on others for decisions, and limited tech comfort. As a result, they remain excluded from the formal financial system.

Our Solution: Samriddhi

A trusted web platform that turns financial learning into real-world action.

  • Step-by-step business toolkits for cash flow, inventory, and pricing
  • Simple bookkeeping that even first-time users can manage
  • Government scheme finder to discover grants and subsidies
  • Risk-free business simulations in Marathi for hands-on learning

Distributed via WhatsApp communities and NGO partnerships for maximum trust and reach.

Why Now

Government programs like the Lakhpati Didi, PM Vishwakarma Yojana, and MSME initiatives are accelerating women-led entrepreneurship. The spread of smartphones, internet access, and local-language AI has made digital learning more accessible than ever. Samriddhi uses these trust rails to make business ownership truly inclusive.

Why Us

We are a cross-functional team in finance, policy, product, engineering, and marketing, united by one goal: empowering women to be financially independent.

  • Clear go-to-market plan
  • Active collaborations with NGOs and banks
  • Sustainable roadmap for growth and impact

Our Vision

To make financial literacy and entrepreneurship as natural as using WhatsApp, so every woman, regardless of literacy or location, can start and grow her own business with confidence.
